# IEEE Xplore and ACM Digital Library — Supplementary Search

**Databases:** IEEE Xplore Digital Library; ACM Digital Library
**Search type:** Supplementary search added in response to peer-review feedback
**Search execution date:** As reported in the manuscript (post-initial-submission supplementary search)

---

## Background

Reviewer 3 requested the inclusion of references from IEEE Xplore and ACM Digital Library — two databases that index computer-science conference proceedings, workshops, and journals. In response, supplementary searches were conducted in these databases using the same three-domain conceptual structure as the primary search.

## Boolean search string

Identical conceptual structure to the primary search:

```
("Frailty" OR "Pre-frailty" OR "Cognitive Frailty" OR "Physical Frailty")
AND
("Machine learning" OR "Artificial Intelligence" OR "Deep learning"
 OR "Predictive Model" OR "Prediction Model" OR "Classification Model")
AND
("Community-dwelling older adults" OR "Community-living older adults"
 OR "Community-dwelling elderly" OR "Older adults living in community")
```

## Eligibility criteria applied

The same prespecified eligibility criteria from the primary search were applied:

1. Community-dwelling adults aged 60 years or older.
2. ML-based frailty prediction or classification.
3. Original full-length research article.
4. Sample size of 1,000 participants or more.
5. SCI(E)-indexed peer-reviewed journal.
6. Non-hospital-based.

## Outcome

After applying the prespecified eligibility criteria:

- **No additional eligible studies were identified** beyond those captured in the primary search of PubMed, Embase, Web of Science, and Scopus.

Most relevant records retrieved from these computer-science databases were:

- Conference proceedings;
- Technical workshop papers; or
- Hospital-based feasibility studies that did not meet the prespecified criteria for community-based public health applicability.

## Methodological rationale

The supplementary search was conducted in good faith to address the reviewer's concern about database coverage. However, expanding the included set to add records that did not meet the prespecified eligibility criteria — particularly conference proceedings, workshop papers, or hospital-based feasibility studies — would have:

- Compromised the integrity of the PROSPERO-registered protocol (CRD420251081555);
- Introduced studies below the threshold of robust prediction-model evidence as defined by Debray et al. (2017) and PROBAST (Wolff et al. 2019); and
- Reduced, rather than enhanced, the methodological rigor of the review.

The supplementary search outcome is documented in Supplementary Table S1 of the manuscript and in this repository so that readers can independently verify the search execution and exclusion reasoning.
